Stakeholder Management Approach

My stakeholder management philosophy comes from EU Parliament experience: put people in the position to discover the problem themselves rather than telling them they have a problem.

At VMware: When facing resistance to video creation, I showed cost-benefit analysis. "Even if this video only works for 1 month but deflects 1,000 contacts, is it worth a few hours of volunteer time?" Let the data make the argument.

At Microsoft: Coordinated Skype sunset across Legal, PR, Product, Marketing, Sales, Customer Service, and partners across multiple countries.

Core principle: Don't convince people your solution is right. Show them how it solves their specific problem.

Innovation Under Constraints

Some of my best work happened with $0 budget. Constraints force creativity.

VMware YouTube Channel: $0 allocated budget. Built volunteer council of ~15 engineers. Used Zoom (already available). Early AI tools for translation. Result: 30K subscribers, 10%+ contact deflection.

The Framework: What do we already have access to? Who is already motivated to help? What's the simplest solution? How do we measure it?

Constraints aren't obstacles. They're forcing functions for finding the essential solution.

Change Management Approach

Change management is about making the new way easier than the old way, then building advocates.

KCS at Blizzard & VMware: I was both practitioner (top 1%) AND change agent. "This methodology helped ME perform at elite level" is more persuasive than "Corporate says do this."

The Pattern: Start with early adopters, prove value through their success, let advocates recruit the next wave, remove friction systematically, celebrate visible wins.

People don't resist change. They resist being changed.

Cross-Functional Coordination

The secret: every stakeholder has their own success criteria and constraints.

Microsoft Skype Sunset: Legal needed compliance. PR needed message control. Product needed migration. Marketing needed comms. Sales needed partners. Support needed readiness.

My Approach: Map everyone's win conditions, identify conflicts early, create shared visibility, establish clear decision-making, hit milestones consistently.

Result: 400 articles retired, 200 redirected, 5 new. 7 departments. Multiple countries. Zero missed deadlines.

Programme Measurement

I'm obsessive about measurement, but the right metrics, not just easy metrics.

At VMware: I pulled from 7 different sources before deciding on the YouTube strategy: community forums, TAM interviews, engineer feedback, UI surveys, Google Analytics, article analysis, Salesforce data.

At Microsoft: Improved content freshness from 67% to 94% in 3 months. I didn't just measure the number. I tracked why certain content wasn't getting refreshed and fixed systemic blockers.

KCS Implementation

I've been a top performer using it, an advocate implementing it, and a programme manager scaling it.

Blizzard (2011-2022): Top 1% EMEA for 5 years. Selected for 6-month strategic assignment during enterprise rollout. 250+ KB articles (EN), 300+ translated (PL).

VMware (2022-2024): Implemented KCS for account support. +35% KB article usage. Roadshows and onboarding for cross-functional teams.

The hardest part isn't the methodology. It's sustaining the cultural shift.
